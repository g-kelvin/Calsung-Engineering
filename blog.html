Importance of Borehole in a Community

  • Admin
  • Jan 1, 2020
  • 12 Comments

Boreholes are an important method of accessing water for irrigation and water supply. A borehole is a narrow hole drilled deep into the ground in order to access a water bearing layer or aquifer. Boreholes are often used to access groundwater when surface water sources are not available or not suitable for use. They are also used to improve water supply in areas with limited surface water resources.

The advantages of using boreholes for irrigation and water supply include the ability to access groundwater which is typically of better quality than surface water sources and which is more reliable in terms of its availability. Boreholes also allow for a large volume of water to be extracted from a single location, which is beneficial when irrigation needs are high or when a large area needs to be supplied with water.

The main disadvantages of boreholes include the cost of drilling and the risk of contamination from surface sources. The cost of drilling a borehole can be high, particularly for deep boreholes. Additionally, boreholes must be carefully monitored to ensure that surface sources of contamination are not entering the aquifer and affecting the quality of the water.

Overall, boreholes can be a useful tool for accessing water for irrigation and water supply, particularly in areas with limited surface water resources or high demand for water. However, their use requires careful consideration of their costs and potential for contamination.
